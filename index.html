Your AI's memory shouldn't be locked to one app. It should follow you everywhere.

Awareness is a portable knowledge store for your life. Tell one AI about your health, your projects, your preferences — and every AI you use knows it. Permanently, portably, privately.

Get started in 5 minutes → See how it works
The problem
Every AI platform keeps its own memory silo. What you teach Claude doesn't exist in ChatGPT. Your desktop assistant's context doesn't follow you to mobile. Switch platforms, and you start over.
What this costs you

You mention a doctor's appointment to your AI on your phone during a commute. Back at your desk, you ask Claude to help schedule a follow-up — it has no idea what you're talking about.

You stored a medication interaction note last week. Today you're asking a different AI about a new supplement — it can't see what you already told the other one.

Every conversation starts from zero. Your knowledge evaporates at the end of each chat.

How it works

Your AI reads a briefing. Every conversation.

A compact summary (~200 tokens) of what matters right now. Intentions that fired, knowledge it needs, things that need attention — or silence, confirming everything was checked.

get_briefing — Claude.ai
{
  "attention_needed": true,
  "intentions": [
    {
      "description": "Schedule dermatology follow-up",
      "state": "fired",
      "deliver_at": "2026-03-27"
    }
  ],
  "knowledge_summary": {
    "total_entries": 342,
    "sources": 26,
    "recent_updates": 8
  },
  "context": [
    "Sleep score 72 (below 7-day avg 81)",
    "CPAP SpO2 dip to 88% at 3:14am"
  ],
  "alerts": [],
  "suppressed": 0,
  "patterns_matched": 0
}
 
// Your AI knows you slept poorly, a reminder fired,
// and 342 knowledge entries are available to query.
// You didn't explain any of it.
Get started

Five minutes.

01

Connect

Run a single command to start awareness. Add the URL to any MCP-compatible AI tool — Claude, Cursor, Claude Code, VS Code, or anything that speaks MCP.

02

Use

Your AIs read a compact briefing at conversation start. Store notes, reminders, personal facts, and project context as you work. Knowledge accumulates through conversation, not configuration.

03

Benefit

Switch tools freely. Knowledge follows you. Reminders fire on schedule. Every AI you use has the same context — no more starting over.

Cross-platform

One brain. Every AI.

Tell your AI about a medication on your phone. Ask about drug interactions on your laptop. Awareness is the portable layer that makes all your AI tools feel like one system.

Use cases

What you can build with it.

📋

Personal knowledge management

Financial accounts, insurance details, medical info, family contacts — tell your AI once, and it’s stored with type, tags, and timestamps. Any AI you use can retrieve it. No re-explaining, ever.

📊

Health & wellness

Sleep scores, CPAP metrics, HRV trends, exercise data — available to any AI you talk to. Track patterns over time. Correlate sleep quality with productivity without explaining your situation every conversation.

🖥️

Homelab monitoring

Your NAS, your Proxmox cluster, your Docker stack — awareness notices when something changes and tells your AI before you ask. The briefing surfaces what matters; silence confirms what's fine.

🔄

Cross-platform development

Start a feature in Claude Code, discuss the design in Claude.ai, review in Cursor. Decisions, project status, and context follow you across every platform without re-explaining.

⏰

Intentions & reminders

“Remind me to call the dentist next week.” Awareness creates an intention that fires automatically at the right time. Your AI surfaces it in your next conversation — on any platform.

🔍

Decision trail

Every entry is timestamped and changelog-tracked. Why did you pick that architecture? What was the reasoning behind that financial decision? The context is preserved at the moment you made the choice.

Knowledge that outlives you

A living estate document.

Over time, your awareness store becomes something profound — financial accounts, insurance details, medical info, system access, family contacts. Not because you sat down to write a manual, but because you asked your AI to remember important details as you mentioned them.

It accumulated as a side effect of living your life with an AI. If you weren't here tomorrow, someone could find what they need.

Vision

The product is silence.

The most important briefing is attention_needed: false — confirmation that everything was checked and nothing needs you. An attention firewall, not another notification source.

Knowledge becomes ambient. It accumulates through daily use, not documentation. A house that remembers when the furnace was serviced. A decision trail that preserves the reasoning at the moment you made the choice.

Personal

One person's knowledge, portable across every AI they use. Working today.

Team

Project knowledge that accumulates through work, not meetings and wikis.

Community

Institutional memory for organizations with zero software budget.

Origin

Born from a single memory instruction.

What started as a homelab monitoring experiment turned into a portable knowledge layer for your entire life. A single line in Claude's memory — “check the NAS health on each conversation” — worked surprisingly well. The AI applied contextual judgment, learned what was normal, and surfaced what wasn't.

But it had obvious limits. Knowledge was locked to one platform. It only worked with one system. Diagnostics weren't captured at detection time.

Awareness is the generalization of that experiment — multi-source, multi-platform, portable knowledge that any AI agent can read and write.
